## Discussion summary

- The kickoff meeting introduced the Faculty Undergraduate Research Practice (FURP) programme as a hands-on undergraduate research programme focused on applying classroom knowledge to real research problems, exploring research interests, and producing academic outputs such as posters, papers, patents, or research demonstrations.
- The programme certificate requirements were clarified. Certificate-eligible participants must satisfy all three conditions: attend more than 50% of programme activities, submit a final poster or presentation PDF, and present at the Poster Showcase either in person or through an arranged stand-in.
- Attendance expectations were emphasized. Weekly meetings, workshops, and scheduled sessions count toward attendance, and both online and in-person participation are valid. Falling below the attendance threshold means the certificate cannot be awarded even if the research work is completed.
- The Research Track requirement was explained. The minimum expectation is to replicate a cited paper and add at least 10% innovation, meaning the project should reproduce existing work and contribute something new such as an extension, comparison, improvement, or additional validation.
- The GitHub workflow was introduced as the main way to track weekly progress. Each student or team should use one repository for the whole programme, keep the required folder structure intact, and update the same repository every week instead of creating new repositories.
- The mandatory repository structure was reviewed: `docs/00_weekly.md` for weekly progress logs, `docs/meeting_notes/` for meeting notes, `src/` for code and experiment materials, and `FURP_Showcase.pdf` in the repository root for the final poster.
- The GitHub tutorial covered how to create a repository from the FURP template, name it according to the required convention, make it visible to supervisors either by adding collaborators or making it public, and commit updates with clear messages.
- The weekly routine was defined: update `docs/00_weekly.md`, add meeting notes in `docs/meeting_notes/`, attend the weekly meeting, and commit the work so supervisors can review progress through GitHub.
- The leave and withdrawal policy was explained. Any leave of absence, withdrawal, or track-switching request must be communicated by email; verbal or chat messages are not sufficient records.
